# Final

**Discipline:** Software Engineering
**Problem type:** Designing a simple client server application with a MySQL database
**Difficulty:** 1 / 5
**Modes:** Practice, Final
**Instability:** Off

## Learning Outcomes

This assessment challenges you to apply the complete software development lifecycle by designing and implementing a full client-server application from concept to completion. 

You will progress through the phases of Frame, Evaluate, Design, Build, Validate, and Reflect, demonstrating both technical proficiency and sound engineering practices at each stage. Your solution will consist of a Java command-line interface (CLI) client that communicates with a Java-based RESTful web service, which in turn persists and retrieves data from a MySQL database.

Throughout the assessment, you will be expected to justify design decisions, implement a robust and maintainable solution, verify that your application meets its requirements through testing and validation, and critically reflect on your development process and the quality of the final product.

The solution must address:

- Justification for use of design patterns (Facade, Factory, Iterator, ...)
- Application of software engineering principles (SOLID, KISS, POLA, ...)
- Consideration of concurrency at all levels
- Use of cryptography for security (Encryption in transit, Hashing passwords, ...)
